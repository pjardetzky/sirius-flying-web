About our program


Sirius Flying LLC provides one-on-one flight and ground training tailored to each individual trainee pilot with emphasis on training busy professionals who demand high standards of experience, professionalism, integrity and efficiency.

We extend the same high standards of instruction to all trainee pilots, whether pursuing a personal goal of becoming a private helicopter pilot or an instrument rated pilot.

Our training allows trainee pilots to enjoy their instructor's undivided attention while having the freedom to set their own type of training schedule and progress at a pace consistent with their individual goals.

We exclusively use Robinson helicopters because they are fast, comfortable, and extremely reliable. Those traits have made Robinson helicopters the most popular training and personal use helicopters in the world. Because of their versatility and low operating cost, Robinson helicopters are an extremely popular choice with private helicopter owners, which many of our trainee pilots aspire to become.

Through our close association with Leading Edge Aviation, Inc., we can assist our trainee pilots in their goal to become Robinson helicopter owners. We also coordinate pilot training to coincide with the delivery of a trainee pilot's new personal helicopter.

Private Pilot License


To obtain a helicopter private pilot certificate, the FAA requires certain minimum requirements be met.

  • You must be at least 17 years of age;
  • You must obtain a FAA medical certificate;;
  • Pass an FAA administered written test on aeroaeronautical knowlege;
  • Log at least 40 hours of flight time with at least 20 hours of flight training from and authorized flight instructor and at least 10 hours of solo flight;
  • Pass an FAA administered oral and flight test;

The details of each requirement are specified, here, in the Federal Aviation Regulations FAR 14 CFR Part 61, Subpart E.

Instrument Rating
